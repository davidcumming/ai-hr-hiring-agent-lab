## 1. Role Overview

The Senior Manager, Digital Health Strategy is responsible for leading strategic initiatives that advance digital health priorities across Canada. The role supports cross-functional collaboration, stakeholder engagement, policy and standards alignment, and the development of strategic recommendations that help accelerate adoption of interoperable digital health solutions.

This role operates in a complex environment involving public-sector partners, health system stakeholders, standards bodies, and internal teams.

## 2. Role Purpose

The purpose of this role is to:

- support the development and execution of strategic digital health initiatives
- lead or coordinate high-impact projects involving multiple stakeholders
- translate strategic priorities into practical plans, deliverables, and recommendations
- support alignment across policy, standards, business, and operational perspectives
- contribute to a collaborative and evidence-based decision environment

## 3. Key Responsibilities

- Lead digital health strategy initiatives and related workstreams
- Develop strategic analyses, briefing materials, recommendations, and planning documents
- Engage stakeholders across jurisdictions, partners, and internal teams
- Support alignment between strategic priorities and implementation realities
- Coordinate cross-functional initiatives involving policy, business, and technical participants
- Identify risks, dependencies, and opportunities affecting program delivery
- Contribute to planning, reporting, and executive-level communications
- Support continuous improvement in how strategic work is organized and delivered

## 4. Required Qualifications

- University degree in health administration, public policy, business, information management, or a related field
- Significant experience in strategy, program leadership, digital health, healthcare transformation, or a related environment
- Demonstrated experience managing complex initiatives involving multiple stakeholders
- Strong written communication and presentation skills
- Strong ability to synthesize complex information and develop clear recommendations
- Experience working in a public-sector, health-sector, or similarly complex governance environment

## 5. Preferred Qualifications

- Experience working with digital health interoperability initiatives
- Experience with pan-Canadian or multi-jurisdictional collaboration
- Familiarity with standards, healthcare information exchange, or implementation guidance
- Experience briefing senior leadership or executive stakeholders
- Bilingualism in English and French

## 6. Core Competencies

- Strategic thinking
- Stakeholder engagement
- Communication
- Judgment
- Planning and coordination
- Leadership and influence
- Analytical thinking
