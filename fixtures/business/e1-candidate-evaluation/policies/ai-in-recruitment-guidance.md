# AI in Recruitment Guidance

## 1. Purpose

This document provides guidance on the appropriate use of AI tools in recruitment support activities. It is intended to help staff use AI in a controlled, transparent, and responsible way.

## 2. Intended Role of AI

AI is a support capability. It may help with drafting, summarization, structuring information, and preparing materials. It is not the accountable decision-maker in hiring.

## 3. Examples of Appropriate Use

AI may be used to support:

- drafting job postings from approved source material
- converting hiring manager notes into a structured intake summary
- proposing screening rubric language
- summarizing resumes against approved criteria
- generating interview questions linked to competencies
- drafting interview scorecards
- formatting shortlist rationale for review

## 4. Examples of Inappropriate Use

AI should not be used to:

- make final hiring decisions
- automatically reject candidates without human review
- apply undisclosed criteria
- use personal, sensitive, or irrelevant attributes as evaluation criteria
- create the appearance that AI output is final or authoritative without validation
- bypass organizational process or approvals

## 5. Human Review Requirement

All AI-generated outputs used in recruitment must be reviewed and approved by an authorized human before being relied upon in the hiring process.

## 6. Transparency Requirement

Where AI materially assisted in preparing a recruitment artifact, the users of that artifact should understand:

- what the AI was used for
- what source information it relied upon
- what assumptions or limitations may apply
- that human review remains required

## 7. Source Quality

AI outputs are only as good as the information provided. Users should prioritize:

- approved templates
- accurate role information
- agreed screening criteria
- current organizational guidance

Unclear or incomplete inputs may result in low-quality outputs.

## 8. Confidentiality and Data Handling

Users should handle candidate information carefully and only use approved systems and approved workflows for sensitive recruitment data.

Prototype or demonstration agents should not be mistaken for production-approved tools.

## 9. Public Sector Considerations

In a public-sector or publicly accountable environment, recruitment-related AI use should be especially attentive to:

- defensibility
- consistency
- fairness
- documentation quality
- privacy
- governance

## 10. Prototype Disclaimer

This guidance may be used in a demonstration or prototype context to illustrate how AI could support recruitment. It does not, by itself, constitute approval for production deployment or live candidate evaluation.
