## 1. Purpose

This document defines the roles and responsibilities of participants in the recruitment process to support clarity, accountability, and consistency.

## 2. Core Roles

The following roles are typically involved in recruitment:

- Human Resources
- Hiring Manager
- Interview Panel Member
- Senior Approver or Executive Approver
- Administrative Support, where applicable

## 3. Human Resources Responsibilities

Human Resources is responsible for enabling and governing the recruitment process.

HR responsibilities include:

- receiving and reviewing hiring requests
- guiding hiring managers through the recruitment process
- supporting role intake and requirements definition
- helping distinguish mandatory and preferred criteria
- reviewing or drafting job postings and related documents
- coordinating posting logistics
- administering candidate communications where applicable
- supporting structured screening practices
- ensuring process documentation is maintained
- supporting fairness, consistency, and defensibility
- advising on appropriate use of AI tools in recruitment support

HR does not replace the hiring manager's accountability for defining business need or making the final hiring recommendation where authority rests with the manager.

## 4. Hiring Manager Responsibilities

The hiring manager owns the business need and is accountable for role-specific content and decision input.

Hiring manager responsibilities include:

- initiating the staffing request
- confirming the purpose and scope of the role
- defining responsibilities and qualifications
- identifying what is truly required versus preferred
- participating in rubric development
- reviewing screening outputs
- participating in shortlisting decisions
- serving on the interview panel or appointing appropriate participants
- making or recommending the final selection decision, as authorized
- ensuring decisions are based on job-related evidence

The hiring manager should not introduce new hidden criteria late in the process without discussion and documentation.

## 5. Interview Panel Member Responsibilities

Interview panel members support structured and consistent candidate assessment.

Their responsibilities include:

- reviewing role requirements and interview materials in advance
- evaluating candidates against agreed criteria
- participating fairly and consistently
- documenting observations and scores
- maintaining confidentiality
- avoiding irrelevant, speculative, or biased considerations
- contributing to evidence-based panel discussions

## 6. Senior Approver Responsibilities

Where required, a senior approver or executive sponsor may be responsible for:

- confirming position authorization
- approving staffing decisions at defined checkpoints
- validating alignment with workforce planning or budget constraints
- approving exceptions or escalations

## 7. Administrative Support Responsibilities

Administrative support may assist with:

- scheduling interviews
- managing document versions
- organizing recruitment materials
- preparing interview packages
- maintaining candidate tracking records

## 8. Decision Rights Summary

### Human Resources
May advise, coordinate, draft, structure, and review.

### Hiring Manager
Defines business need, confirms criteria, participates in assessment, and makes or recommends final selection where authorized.

### Interview Panel
Assesses and documents observations but does not act independently outside the agreed process.

### AI Tools
May support drafting, summarization, formatting, and structured analysis but do not make final decisions.

## 9. Accountability Principles

### 9.1 Human Accountability
Final decisions remain with authorized human participants.

### 9.2 Documented Rationale
Material decisions should be supported by documented rationale.

### 9.3 Consistent Application of Criteria
Agreed criteria should be applied consistently across candidates.

### 9.4 Confidentiality
Candidate information must only be shared with those who need it for recruitment purposes.

## 10. AI-Supported Recruitment Clarification

Where AI is used in recruitment support, responsibilities remain unchanged:

- HR remains responsible for process support and governance
- the hiring manager remains responsible for role decisions
- interviewers remain responsible for their assessments
- humans remain responsible for reviewing and approving AI-generated outputs

AI should be treated as a support tool, not a decision-maker.
