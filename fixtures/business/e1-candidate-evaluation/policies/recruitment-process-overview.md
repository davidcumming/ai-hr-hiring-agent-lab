## 1. Purpose

This document defines the standard end-to-end recruitment process used to attract, assess, and hire qualified candidates in a fair, consistent, transparent, and timely manner.

The process is designed to:

- support merit-based hiring
- promote fairness and consistency across competitions
- ensure hiring decisions are evidence-based and defensible
- align recruitment activities with organizational values, workforce planning, and operational needs
- support privacy, confidentiality, and appropriate handling of candidate information

## 2. Scope

This process applies to:

- permanent full-time positions
- permanent part-time positions
- contract roles
- temporary assignments
- backfill or replacement positions
- net new positions approved through workforce planning

This process applies to Human Resources, hiring managers, interview panel members, and other internal participants involved in recruitment.

## 3. Guiding Principles

Recruitment activities shall be conducted according to the following principles:

### 3.1 Fairness
All candidates shall be assessed using job-related criteria applied consistently across the competition.

### 3.2 Transparency
The role requirements, process stages, and decision rationale should be sufficiently documented to support review and explanation.

### 3.3 Merit
Selection decisions should be based on qualifications, relevant experience, competencies, and demonstrated ability to perform the role.

### 3.4 Privacy and Confidentiality
Candidate information shall be collected, accessed, shared, and retained only as necessary for recruitment purposes and in accordance with applicable organizational policies and legal obligations.

### 3.5 Inclusion
Recruitment processes should be designed to reduce unnecessary barriers and support equitable access for qualified applicants.

### 3.6 Efficiency
The process should be timely, organized, and proportionate to the complexity and seniority of the role.

## 4. Recruitment Process Summary

The standard recruitment lifecycle includes the following stages:

1. Workforce need identified
2. Hiring request initiated
3. Role definition and intake
4. Job description and posting prepared
5. Posting approval obtained
6. Position posted and promoted
7. Applications received
8. Initial screening completed
9. Longlist or shortlist prepared
10. Interviews and assessments conducted
11. References and final checks completed
12. Selection decision made
13. Offer prepared and issued
14. Recruitment file closed and retained

## 5. Standard Process Stages

## 5.1 Workforce Need Identification

The hiring manager identifies a staffing need based on one or more of the following:

- vacancy due to resignation, transfer, or leave
- approved new role
- capacity gap
- new initiative or project
- specialized skills requirement

The hiring manager confirms that recruitment is the appropriate response and that budget, role approval, and organizational need are established.

## 5.2 Hiring Request Initiation

The hiring manager submits a hiring request to Human Resources. The request should include:

- position title
- department or team
- reporting relationship
- employment type
- business rationale
- target timing
- funding confirmation
- whether the role is net new, replacement, or temporary

HR reviews the request for completeness and confirms readiness to proceed.

## 5.3 Role Definition and Intake

HR conducts an intake discussion with the hiring manager to confirm the role and hiring strategy.

The intake should establish:

- purpose of the role
- key responsibilities
- required qualifications
- preferred qualifications
- relevant technical and behavioral competencies
- disqualifying criteria
- language or communication requirements
- work arrangement expectations
- interview panel composition
- desired assessment methods

At this stage, HR and the hiring manager should also confirm the distinction between:

- mandatory requirements
- asset qualifications
- competencies to assess in interview
- criteria that should not be used in screening

## 5.4 Job Description and Posting Preparation

HR and the hiring manager prepare or refine the job description and job posting.

The posting should include:

- role title
- organizational context
- primary responsibilities
- required qualifications
- preferred qualifications
- employment type
- location or work arrangement
- application deadline
- accessibility statement
- any special instructions for applying

Where possible, standard templates should be used.

## 5.5 Posting Approval

Before posting, the following should be confirmed:

- role content is accurate
- screening criteria are job-related
- salary range or classification is validated
- required internal approvals are in place
- posting language is clear and consistent

## 5.6 Posting and Outreach

The role is posted through approved channels, which may include:

- internal career page
- external job boards
- professional networks
- LinkedIn
- targeted outreach where appropriate

Posting duration should reflect the nature of the role, urgency, and labor market conditions.

## 5.7 Application Receipt and Administration

HR receives and organizes candidate applications. HR may perform an administrative review to identify:

- incomplete applications
- duplicate applications
- submissions received after deadline, if relevant
- obvious non-responsiveness to mandatory requirements

Administrative review should not replace substantive qualification screening.

## 5.8 Initial Screening

Applications are screened against pre-established criteria.

Screening should:

- use a documented rubric or structured criteria set
- distinguish required from preferred qualifications
- identify clear disqualifiers
- capture rationale for inclusion, exclusion, or uncertainty
- avoid relying on one-line conclusions without evidence

The output of this stage is typically:

- screened out
- possible fit / needs further review
- strong fit / shortlist candidate

## 5.9 Shortlist Preparation

HR and the hiring manager review screening results and confirm which candidates advance.

Shortlisting decisions should be supported by documented rationale, particularly where judgment is required.

## 5.10 Interviews and Assessments

The interview stage may include:

- structured interview
- technical exercise
- written assignment
- case study
- presentation
- panel discussion

Assessment methods should be proportional to the role and should evaluate relevant competencies.

Interviewers should use a common evaluation form and score candidates independently where practical before discussing results as a panel.

## 5.11 References and Final Checks

References may be conducted for final candidate(s) in accordance with organizational practice.

Other checks may include, where relevant and authorized:

- credential verification
- conflict checks
- background verification
- work eligibility checks

Only checks appropriate to the role and approved by policy should be performed.

## 5.12 Selection Decision

The final decision is made by the authorized hiring decision-maker based on the full body of evidence, which may include:

- application materials
- screening results
- interview performance
- assignments or tests
- references
- job-related organizational considerations

The rationale should be documented.

## 5.13 Offer and Closing

HR prepares the offer and coordinates communications.

Once the process is complete:

- the recruitment file is finalized
- records are stored according to retention practices
- lessons learned may be captured for future improvement

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### Human Resources
HR is responsible for:

- guiding the process
- ensuring procedural consistency
- supporting the hiring manager
- preparing or reviewing recruitment materials
- coordinating posting and administration
- maintaining documentation
- supporting fair and defensible process execution

### Hiring Manager
The hiring manager is responsible for:

- defining business need
- confirming role requirements
- participating in screening and assessment
- providing timely decisions
- making the final hiring recommendation where authorized

### Interview Panel Members
Panel members are responsible for:

- reviewing materials in advance
- assessing candidates against agreed criteria
- documenting observations
- maintaining confidentiality
- avoiding irrelevant or inappropriate considerations

## 7. Documentation Requirements

At minimum, the following should be retained in the recruitment file:

- hiring request
- approved job description or posting
- screening criteria or rubric
- shortlist rationale
- interview questions and scorecards
- interview notes or summary evaluations
- final decision rationale
- offer documentation
- any other required administrative records

## 8. Use of AI in Recruitment Support

AI tools may be used to support administrative and analytical aspects of recruitment, such as:

- drafting job postings
- structuring screening criteria
- summarizing resumes against approved rubrics
- generating interview questions
- formatting evaluation materials

AI tools must not:

- make final hiring decisions
- automatically reject applicants without human review
- apply unlawful, non-job-related, or unapproved criteria
- replace accountable human judgment

All AI-supported outputs must be reviewed and validated by authorized human participants before use.

## 9. Exceptions

Any significant departure from this process should be discussed with Human Resources and documented where appropriate.

## 10. Review and Continuous Improvement

This process should be reviewed periodically to improve:

- fairness
- consistency
- timeliness
- candidate experience
- hiring manager experience
- quality of documentation
- appropriate use of enabling technology
