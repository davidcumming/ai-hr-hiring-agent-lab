# 40.8 Privacy, Data Residency, and Auditability Policy

> **Status:** Stub — not yet authored; see §40 of the Process Doc (`gentech_slice_based_development_process_revised.md`, §40.8). Required before first project development if the project may involve healthcare, public-sector, sensitive, regulated, PHI, PII, or confidential client data. If the first project is purely non-sensitive and exploratory, this document may start as a short policy stub and be expanded before sensitive data is introduced.
> **Purpose:** Instantiates §23 for the project — eval data governance, synthetic/de-identified data expectations, production-data restrictions, Azure/Foundry data-residency expectations, and audit-archive, access-control, and retention requirements.

## Where the content currently lives

- **§23** of `gentech_slice_based_development_process_revised.md` — the privacy / residency / auditability policy this document instantiates.
- **40.2 §13 (Auditability), §14 (Observability), §15 (Data residency)** — the architecture-side controls this policy must align with: evidence ledger vs. telemetry separation, immutability posture, single declared region of record, co-located stores, residency-honouring Foundry endpoints, and no silent cross-border movement.
- **[ADR-0004 — Canadian data-residency verification](adr/ADR-0004-canadian-residency-verification.md)** — the two per-capability residency checks (model availability in Canadian Foundry regions; Copilot Studio cross-geo data movement) that this policy must require for sensitive-data projects.
- Related: R17 (enable Blob versioning + time-based immutability on evidence from slice 1) is the lab-default immutability posture this policy should adopt.

## What this document must define when authored

The project's privacy, residency, and auditability policy per §23: eval data governance, synthetic/de-identified-by-default data expectations, production/personal-data restrictions, the declared Azure/Foundry region of record and approved exceptions, and the audit-archive, access-control, and retention requirements — incorporating the ADR-0004 residency checks where sensitive data is in scope.
